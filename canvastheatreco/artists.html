Artists

Rosanna Elliott

Artistic Director

Rosanna Elliott is a director with a passion for making theatre that engages new audiences and challenges our ideals as a society.

With a particular interest in set design and art history, Rosanna is currently exploring how theatre makers can engage with cutting edge design and technology in order to enhance the storytelling process in an accessible way. Working with her colleague Ciaran Clarke, she recently set up Canvas Theatre with this project in mind.

Rosanna has a lot of experience working with text and specialises in directing both adaptations of classic plays and new writing. This year she rdirected the Nick Darke adaptation of Laurie Lee’s Cider with Rosie for Trebiggan productions at The Minack Theatre. The show consisted of a cast of six plus a live original score from the People’s String Foundation.

Rosanna has recently returned from working for the RSC as Assistant Director on a piece of New Writing in development as part of their project ‘The Other Place’. In 2014 she was Assistant Director on Headlong Theatre’s UK tour of 1984 created by Robert Icke and Duncan Macmillan. She also worked on Ria Parry’s The Girl Who Never Looked Up at the National Theatre Krietman Studio.

She regularly directs pieces of new writing for the for the successful Etch Scratch night in London and has recently set up a brand new Scratch Night in Cornwall.

She is a member of the Young Vic Genesis Directors Network and has ten years involvement with the National Youth Theatre of Great Britain. The companies that she has devised work with include the NYT, Kneehigh and Trailblaze. As an actor, Rosanna previously took work to the Edinburgh Fringe. Her experience also includes Film and Television. In 2012, she was Crowd AD on feature film Summer in February.

Rosanna’s love for theatre is apparent in every project that she is involved in. Her First Class BA Hons in Drama from the University of Exeter was enhanced by a commendation for ‘Contribution to the Life of the Department’. While studying at Exeter, she directed adaptations of A Midsummer Night’s Dream and A Streetcar Named Desire.

With a line of dynamic projects being planned with Canvas, Rosanna is incredibly excited about the potential of the next few years.

Ciaran Clarke

Artistic Director

Ciaran is an actor, writer and director, passionate about fusing performance and technology.

He was heavily involved in Miracle Theatre’s live streaming NESTA project, and worked closely with BT, Falmouth University and Miracle on the Distributed Tempest in September 2014. He has been touring as an actor with Miracle Theatre since May 2012, as well as fulfilling additional roles of touring technician and technical designer. He has also worked with Kneehigh, o-region and Blackfish.

Ciaran read Drama and Theatre Studies at Trinity College Dublin, and studied Advanced Playwriting under Irish writer Marina Carr, graduating in 2010. Later that year, he became General Manager of The Poly, Falmouth, working with the board of trustees to save the venue from closure.

He won an Irish Student Drama Award for Sound Design in 2010 and runs Near-ta Theatre, a Falmouth-based new writing company, producing original work since 2004.

Millie Jones

Associate Producer

Millie is a theatre-maker, who has accrued a wealth of experience in theatre, film and events within the last year since moving back to Cornwall. She has worked with a variety of companies, including o-region, cube and Cscape Dance Company, as producer, project manager, tour assistant and art department assistant, as well as stage managing Trebiggan’s production of Cider with Rosie at the Minack Theatre.

She is currently working as administrator for WildWorks, as well as stage manager on their Christmas show at the Eden Project. From moor to rainforest to cliff-top theatre, Millie can be found in her walking boots, notebook in hand, ready for any challenge.

Myriddin Wannell

Production Designer

Myriddin Wannell (Mydd) is a Production Designer with over 10 years of professional experience. He is based in Cornwall – but in keeping with his Romany roots – he works in a variety of places nationally and internationally. Mydd has two degrees - a BA Theatre Design from Wimbledon School of Art and a BA Fine Art from Falmouth College of Art.

With a collaborative nature and an urge to play, his work has seen him shape the look and feel of theatre, live art, dance, opera and film. Whether it’s Theatre Square at the National, the pit at The Barbican, a castle in Belgium or a palace in London, biomes in Cornwall or the streets of Palestine, he balances big ideas with attention to detail, creating visual worlds that seamlessly respond to space, character and story.

He is currently exhibiting work from the Passion as part of Make Believe: UK Design for Performance 2011 – 2015 at the V&A Museum in London. The exhibition runs until January 2016.

Myriddin is Associate Designer of WildWorks (working with the company since it was formed in 2005) and is a Design Consultant for The Eden Project.

Alfie Tiley

Consultant Astrophysicist

Alfie Tiley is a D.Phil student in the Astrophysics sub-department of the University of Oxford, studying galaxy evolution. His research focuses on how galaxies have evolved from the epoch of peak global star formation at redshifts of z~1-3, around 8 billion years ago, to the present day. He is a member of the K-band Multi Object Spectrograph (KMOS) Redshift One Spectroscopic Survey (KROSS) - a collaborative undertaking between Durham University and University of Oxford to study the spatially resolved kinematics and chemical properties of ~1000 galaxies as they were when the Universe was half its current age.

In separate work he combines integrated radio observations with other photometric data in order to study the traits of dark and luminous matter in local galaxies. Before coming to Oxford, Alfie completed an MSci Physics with Astrophysics at the University of Bristol, graduating with First Class Honours.
